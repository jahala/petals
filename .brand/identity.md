## Brand Story

Most companies do not fail because they lack ideas. They fail because their ideas grow in different directions. The roadmap drifts from the strategy. The brand says different things in different places. Customer signals sit in one tool, competitor signals in another, and decisions are made, forgotten, repeated, or contradicted.

plotplot exists for teams that need more than another dashboard. It gives the company a living map: what it believes, what it is building, how it speaks, what the market is signaling, and where the work is beginning to sprawl.

The product language comes from cultivation because coherent companies are grown, not assembled. Ideas are planted, roadmaps are tended, weak work is pruned, launches bloom, and outcomes are harvested into memory.

plotplot is not only a planning tool, a brand tool, or a monitoring tool. It is a company coherence platform: a connected operating layer for teams that want to grow what matters and stop feeding what does not.

## Mark (evolved 2026-06-12)

Three slim petals fanned from one base point — vital leaf `#3F7A33`, vivid blossom `#C04B3C`, luminous petal `#E8917F` — beside the lowercase wordmark "petals" set whole in ink. The mark carries the palette itself; the word stays calm. It echoes the three skills (extract / distribute / check) and says the name: petals, plural. Replaces the single-petal mark, which replaced the four-petal pinwheel.

## Logo Usage

- **Canonical asset**: `.brand/assets/logo.svg`. Copy it byte-for-byte — never redraw, trace, or approximate the paths.
- **Fills are exact**: `#3F7A33` / `#C04B3C` / `#E8917F`, in that drawing order. Recoloring the mark is off-brand. One exception: an all-ink (`#20241B`) mono variant where color is unavailable (favicons on unknown surfaces, single-color print).
- **Minimum size**: 20px mark height in digital use. Below that, use the mono variant or the wordmark alone.
- **Clear space**: at least half the mark's height on all sides.
- **Never**: stretch, rotate, outline, add shadows or effects, place on backgrounds that fail the contrast pairings in `colors.md`, or use a single petal of the fan as the logo.
- **Wordmark**: lowercase "petals", set whole in the heading serif, ink. Never split, never letter-spaced apart, never title-cased.
